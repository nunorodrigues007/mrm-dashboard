The ice is not breaking. But it is measurably thinner. Mandatory posture: defensive bias, quality tilt, elevated cash buffer, no leverage.

Pillar Deep Dive — I: Cycle (+1.0 WoW — Biggest Mover)

This week's most significant development is the deterioration in the Cycle Pillar, which scored +1.0 WoW from 4.5 to 5.5. The 10Y-2Y spread compressed from +0.55% to +0.46% — moving in the wrong direction. The yield curve is re-flattening after its post-inversion steepening, which historically signals that the market is re-pricing recession risk rather than growth recovery.

The critical threshold to monitor is +0.30%. A compression to that level would trigger a Cycle score of 6.0+ and push the Global Resilience Score toward 7.0 — approaching the upper boundary of the Turbulence Regime and the threshold for a Fracture Regime reassessment. Combined with an ERP already in Red Alert, a Cycle deterioration of this magnitude would represent a multi-pillar convergence event — the most dangerous configuration in the framework's historical analysis.
